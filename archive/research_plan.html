Carbon-Intelligent Workload Scheduling for AI Data Centers

A Deterministic Optimization Approach Across Temporal and Spatial Dimensions

Student Yaxin (Isabel) Wu Supervisor Prof. Bissan Ghaddar Program Master of Business Analytics & Big Data Duration 8 Weeks · May – June 2026

Core Research Question

How can a deterministic mathematical programming model — driven by carbon intensity (CI), renewable energy fraction (RF), and CI variability (CV) — minimize AI data center carbon emissions through joint temporal (when to run) and spatial (where to run) workload scheduling across five grid regions?

Core Indicators

CI

Carbon Intensity

Primary optimization signal in the objective function. Unit: gCO₂eq/kWh. Fully comparable across regions.

↳ ElectricityMaps API · carbonIntensity field

RF

Renewable Fraction

Distinguishes stable clean energy (nuclear) from variable clean energy (wind/solar) when CI values are equal, enabling smarter scheduling.

↳ ElectricityMaps API · powerBreakdown field (wind + solar + hydro)

CV

CI Variability

Measures temporal scheduling opportunity per region (std / mean of CI). Explains cross-region result differences — high CV = high savings potential.

↳ Computed from historical CI time series — no additional data source needed

$

Electricity Price (Constraint)

Per-region budget cap. Absolute prices not compared across regions, preserving model generalizability.

↳ Future work: upgrade to objective with regional market mechanisms (PJM LMP, Nord Pool, USEP)

Optimization Model — Unified Joint LP

Decision Variable

xr,t ≥ 0 — compute load in region r at hour t

A single LP jointly optimizes when and where to run workloads across all 5 regions and all time periods simultaneously. The model allocates load freely across the r×t space to find the globally carbon-optimal schedule.

min Σ_r Σ_t x_{r,t} · CI_r(t) · (1 − α · RF_r(t))

s.t. (C1) Σ_r Σ_t x_{r,t} = D               [demand completion · Liu et al., 2012]
     (C2) x_{r,t} ≤ C_max,r                [regional capacity · Liu et al., 2012]
     (C3) x_{flex,r,t} ≤ VCC_r(t)         [VCC constraint · Colangelo et al., 2025]
     (C4) Σ_t x_{r,t}·P_r(t) ≤ Budget_r  [electricity budget · Lin et al., 2026]
